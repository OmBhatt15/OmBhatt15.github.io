Project JAEGAR

Bio-Mimetic Robotics High-Current Power Distribution SolidWorks CAD

An anthropomorphic robotic arm designed to replicate human motion in real-time. The primary goal was to create a fluid, high-torque system capable of mimicking delicate hand gestures for potential medical and teleoperation applications. The end goal is to create a full scale humanoid robot that can mimic a user's actions.

Phase 1: Finger Actuation Iterations
Phase 2: Full Hand Assembly

The Challenge

The main difficulty was handling the power surge when all 5 fingers actuated simultaneously. Standard microcontrollers couldn't handle the current draw, leading to brownouts. Additionally, mapping linear servo movement to the non-linear rotation of human finger joints required complex mechanical linkage design.

Engineering Solution

I designed a dedicated power distribution bus separate from the logic controller to isolate noise and manage high loads. For the mechanics, I utilized SolidWorks to iterate on a "tendon-driven" design, using high-tension nylon string routed through 3D-printed phalanges to simulate muscle contraction.

Project SCORPION

Inverse Kinematics PCB Design Autonomous Navigation

A hexapod robot built to assist with daily tasks, surveillance and exploration. This platform served as a testbed for learning inverse kinematics algorithms and custom PCB fabrication.

Design Evolution: Leg Mechanisms
Chassis & Integration

The Challenge

Coordinate 18 separate servos (3 per leg) to achieve a smooth "tripod gait" walking cycle. Traditional breadboard wiring was too bulky and unreliable for a moving chassis, causing frequent connection failures during movement. The actual mechanical design of the robot was very tedious to get reliable and upright. Current issue is understanding autonomous movement and adding the scorpion claw to assist with tasks.

Engineering Solution

I developed a custom Inverse Kinematics solver in Python, with the help of AI, to calculate the precise angle of every joint based on a desired foot coordinate (x,y,z). To solve the wiring issue, I designed a custom PCB in KiCad that integrated the servo drivers and power management directly into the robot's chassis, reducing weight by 20%. We underwent 3 iterations of the robot's leg design, ending with an ultralight but sturdy design.

Project SENTINEL

Computer Vision Python / OpenCV Real-Time Tracking

An automated defense simulation system using computer vision. Designed to track aerial targets and align a turret mechanism in real-time without human input.

The Challenge

Processing video feeds fast enough to track a moving object while simultaneously driving stepper motors. Latency between the camera detecting an object and the motors moving created a "lag" that made the tracking inaccurate.

Engineering Solution

I implemented, with the help of AI, a multi-threaded Python script where one thread handled the Heavy OpenCV image processing, and a separate lightweight thread handled the motor control. This allowed the turret to predict the target's path (vector analysis) rather than just reacting to where it was, significantly improving accuracy.
